Spring 2016 - Problem 7

Galois theory

Show that for every positive integer nnn, there exists a cyclic extension of Q\QQ of degree nnn which is contained in R\RR.
Solution.

By Dirichlet's theorem, we may find a prime ppp such that p≡1mod  2np \equiv 1 \mod 2np≡1mod2n. Let ζp\zeta_pζp​ be a primitive ppp-th root of unity so that Q(ζp)\Q\p{\zeta_p}Q(ζp​) has Galois group Cp−1C_{p-1}Cp−1​. Note that complex conjugation is an automorphism of Q(ζp)\Q\p{\zeta_p}Q(ζp​) fixing Q\QQ, so let C2C_2C2​ be the subgroup generated by this.

Since Cp−1C_{p-1}Cp−1​ is abelian, C2C_2C2​ is a normal subgroup, so the field EEE fixed by C2C_2C2​ is Galois over Q\QQ with Galois group Cp−1/C2≃C(p−1)/2C_{p-1}/C_2 \simeq C_{\p{p-1}/2}Cp−1​/C2​≃C(p−1)/2​. Moreover, E⊆RE \subseteq \RE⊆R since it is fixed by complex conjugation. Finally, by choice of ppp, we know nnn divides p−12\frac{p-1}{2}2p−1​, so let k=p−12nk = \frac{p-1}{2n}k=2np−1​. As before, CkC_kCk​ is a normal subgroup of C(p−1)/2C_{\p{p-1}/2}C(p−1)/2​ whose fixed field FFF is Galois over Q\QQ with Galois group C(p−1)/2/Ck≃CnC_{\p{p-1}/2}/C_k \simeq C_nC(p−1)/2​/Ck​≃Cn​. Since F⊆E⊆RF \subseteq E \subseteq \RF⊆E⊆R, we are done.
