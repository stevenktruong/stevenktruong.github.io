Fall 2016 - Problem 2

Let KKK be a semi-simple quadratic extension over Q\QQ and consider the regular representation ρ ⁣:K→M2(Q)\func{\rho}{K}{M_2\p{\Q}}ρ:K→M2​(Q). Compute the index of ρ(K×)\rho\p{K^\times}ρ(K×) in the normalizer of ρ(K×)\rho\p{K^\times}ρ(K×) in GL⁡2(Q)\GL_2\p{\Q}GL2​(Q), and justify your answer.
Solution.

By Artin-Wedderburn, KKK is a product of at most two matrix algebras: K≃Mn1(D1)×Mn2(D2)K \simeq M_{n_1}\p{D_1} \times M_{n_2}\p{D_2}K≃Mn1​​(D1​)×Mn2​​(D2​). Since KKK is a two-dimensional Q\QQ-algebra, we have n12dim⁡D1+n22dim⁡D2=2n_1^2 \dim{D_1} + n_2^2 \dim{D_2} = 2n12​dimD1​+n22​dimD2​=2.

Without loss of generality, suppose n1=1n_1 = 1n1​=1 so either dim⁡D1=2\dim{D_1} = 2dimD1​=2 and n2=0n_2 = 0n2​=0, or dim⁡D1=dim⁡D2=n2=1\dim{D_1} = \dim{D_2} = n_2 = 1dimD1​=dimD2​=n2​=1. In the first case, KKK is a field since KKK is a two-dimensional algebra and Q\QQ is commutative, so KKK is a two-dimensional field extension, i.e., K≃Q(d)K \simeq \Q\p{\sqrt{d}}K≃Q(d​) for some non-square d∈Qd \in \Qd∈Q. In the second case, we simply have K≃Q×QK \simeq \Q \times \QK≃Q×Q.

Case 2: K≃Q×QK \simeq \Q \times \QK≃Q×Q
