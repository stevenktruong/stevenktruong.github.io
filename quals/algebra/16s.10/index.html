Spring 2016 - Problem 10

Galois theory
  1. Determine the Galois group of the polynomial X4−2X^4 - 2X4−2 over Q\QQ, as a subgroup of a permutation group. Also, give generators and relations for this group.
  2. Determine the Galois group of the polynomial X3−3X−1X^3 - 3X - 1X3−3X−1 over Q\QQ. (Hint: for polynomials of the form X3+aX+bX^3 + aX + bX3+aX+b, the quantity Δ=−4a3−27b2\Delta = -4a^3 - 27b^2Δ=−4a3−27b2, known as the discriminant, plays a key theoretical role.) Explain your answer.
Solution.

  1. Over C\CC, the roots of f=X4−2f = X^4 - 2f=X4−2 are 21/4,i21/4,−21/42^{1/4}, i2^{1/4}, -2^{1/4}21/4,i21/4,−21/4, and −i21/4-i2^{1/4}−i21/4, so the splitting field of fff is Q(21/4,i)\Q\p{2^{1/4}, i}Q(21/4,i). Consider the tower

    Q⊆Q(21/4)⊆Q(21/4,i).\Q \subseteq \Q\p{2^{1/4}} \subseteq \Q\p{2^{1/4}, i}.Q⊆Q(21/4)⊆Q(21/4,i).

    Note that [Q(21/4):Q]=4\br{\Q\p{2^{1/4}} : \Q} = 4[Q(21/4):Q]=4 since X4−2X^4 - 2X4−2 is irreducible by Eisenstein with p=2p = 2p=2. For the second inclusion, note that X2+1X^2 + 1X2+1 is still irreducible over Q(21/4)\Q\p{2^{1/4}}Q(21/4), since i∉Q(21/4)⊆Ri \notin \Q\p{2^{1/4}} \subseteq \Ri∈/Q(21/4)⊆R, so [Q(21/4,i):Q]=8\br{\Q\p{2^{1/4}, i} : \Q} = 8[Q(21/4,i):Q]=8.

    The maps σ ⁣:21/4↦i21/4\sigma\colon 2^{1/4} \mapsto i2^{1/4}σ:21/4↦i21/4 and τ ⁣:i↦−i\tau\colon i \mapsto -iτ:i↦−i are all field automorphisms fixing Q\QQ. Moreover, these satisfy the relation σ4=τ2=στστ=id⁡\sigma^4 = \tau^2 = \sigma\tau\sigma\tau = \idσ4=τ2=στστ=id. Thus, ⟨σ,τ⟩=D4\gen{\sigma, \tau} = D_4⟨σ,τ⟩=D4​ in the Galois group, but because Q(21/4,i)/Q\Q\p{2^{1/4}, i}/\QQ(21/4,i)/Q is Galois, it follows that Gal⁡(Q(21/4,i))≃D4≤S4\Gal\p{\Q\p{2^{1/4}, i}} \simeq D_4 \leq S_4Gal(Q(21/4,i))≃D4​≤S4​.

  2. Notice that by the rational roots theorem, the only possible roots in Q\QQ are 111 and −1-1−1, which are not roots. Thus, f=X3−3X−1f = X^3 - 3X - 1f=X3−3X−1 has no roots in Q\QQ, hence irreducible as any reducible cubic polynomial will have a linear factor.

    The discriminant of fff is Δ=−4⋅(−3)3−27⋅(−1)2=81=92\Delta = -4 \cdot \p{-3}^3 - 27 \cdot \p{-1}^2 = 81 = 9^2Δ=−4⋅(−3)3−27⋅(−1)2=81=92. Thus, any σ\sigmaσ in the Galois group GGG of fff fixes Δ=9\sqrt{\Delta} = 9Δ​=9, so G≤A3G \leq A_3G≤A3​. We deduce ∣G∣≤3\abs{G} \leq 3∣G∣≤3, but because fff is irreducible, we also have ∣G∣≥3\abs{G} \geq 3∣G∣≥3, so ∣G∣=3\abs{G} = 3∣G∣=3, hence G≃C3G \simeq C_3G≃C3​.
