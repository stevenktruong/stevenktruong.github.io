Fall 2009 - Problem 9

meromorphic functions, periodic functions, residue theorem

Let fff be a non-constant meromorphic function on the complex plane C\CC that obeys

f(z)=f(z+2)=f(z+i2).f\p{z} = f\p{z + \sqrt{2}} = f\p{z + i\sqrt{2}}.f(z)=f(z+2​)=f(z+i2​).

(In particular, the poles of these three functions coincide.) Assume fff has at most one pole in the closed unit disc

D‾={z∣∣z∣≤1}.\cl{D} = \set{z \mid \abs{z} \leq 1}.D={z∣∣z∣≤1}.
  1. Prove that fff has exactly one pole in D‾\cl{D}D.
  2. Prove that this is not a simple pole.
Solution.

  1. Notice that the conditions imply that fff is double periodic, so the behavior of fff in the rectangle R=[0,2]×[0,2]R = \br{0, \sqrt{2}} \times \br{0, \sqrt{2}}R=[0,2​]×[0,2​] determine the behavior of fff globally.

    Suppose first that fff has no poles in RRR. Since RRR is compact, this means that fff is bounded on RRR, which means by double periodicity that fff is bounded on all of C\CC. By Lioville's theorem, this implies fff is constant, which is impossible. Thus, fff must have at least one pole z0∈Rz_0 \in Rz0​∈R.

    By symmetry, the point furthest from every vertex of RRR must be the center 12+i2\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} + \frac{i}{\sqrt{2}}2​1​+2​i​, which has distance 111 from every vertex. Let vvv be a closest vertex to z0z_0z0​ so that ∣z0−v∣≤1\abs{z_0 - v} \leq 1∣z0​−v∣≤1.   2. Consider a different rectangle R=[−12,12]×[−12,12]R = \br{-\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}, \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}} \times \br{-\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}, \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}}R=[−2​1​,2​1​]×[−2​1​,2​1​]. Indeed, R⊆D‾R \subseteq \cl{D}R⊆D, so by periodicity, if there is a pole on one of the sides, then there will be another pole on the opposite side, but D‾\cl{D}D has exactly one pole, which is impossible.

    Let γL,γB,γR,γT\gamma_L, \gamma_B, \gamma_R, \gamma_TγL​,γB​,γR​,γT​ be the left, bottom, right, top side of ∂R\partial{R}∂R oriented counter-clockwise, respectively.     ∫γLf(z) dz=−∫γRf(z) dzand∫γBf(z) dz=−∫γTf(z) dz.\int_{\gamma_L} f\p{z} \,\diff{z} = -\int_{\gamma_R} f\p{z} \,\diff{z} \quad\text{and}\quad \int_{\gamma_B} f\p{z} \,\diff{z} = -\int_{\gamma_T} f\p{z} \,\diff{z}.∫γL​​f(z)dz=−∫γR​​f(z)dzand∫γB​​f(z)dz=−∫γT​​f(z)dz.
