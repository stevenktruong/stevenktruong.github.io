measure theory

Let XXX and YYY be topological spaces and X×YX \times YX×Y the Cartesian product endowed with the product topology. B(X)\mathcal{B}\p{X}B(X) denotes the Borel sets in XXX and similarly, B(Y)\mathcal{B}\p{Y}B(Y) and B(X×Y)\mathcal{B}\p{X \times Y}B(X×Y).

  1. Suppose f ⁣:X→Y\func{f}{X}{Y}f:X→Y is continuous. Prove that E∈B(Y)E \in \mathcal{B}\p{Y}E∈B(Y) implies f−1(E)∈B(X)f^{-1}\p{E} \in \mathcal{B}\p{X}f−1(E)∈B(X).
  2. Suppose A∈B(X)A \in \mathcal{B}\p{X}A∈B(X) and E∈B(Y)E \in \mathcal{B}\p{Y}E∈B(Y). Show that A×E∈B(X×Y)A \times E \in \mathcal{B}\p{X \times Y}A×E∈B(X×Y).

    XXX is open, so X∈FX \in \mathcal{F}X∈F.

    Given E∈FE \in \mathcal{F}E∈F, if x∈(f−1(E))cx \in \p{f^{-1}\p{E}}^\compx∈(f−1(E))c, then f(x)∈Ecf\p{x} \in E^\compf(x)∈Ec, so x∈f−1(Ec)x \in f^{-1}\p{E^\comp}x∈f−1(Ec).     Finally, given {En}n⊆F\set{E_n}_n \subseteq \mathcal{F}{En​}n​⊆F,

    x∈f−1(⋃n=1∞En)  ⟺  ∃n:f(x)∈En  ⟺  ∃n:x∈f−1(En)  ⟺  x∈⋃n=1∞f−1(En),\begin{aligned} x \in f^{-1}\p{\bigcup_{n=1}^\infty E_n} &\iff \exists n : f\p{x} \in E_n \\ &\iff \exists n : x \in f^{-1}\p{E_n} \\ &\iff x \in \bigcup_{n=1}^\infty f^{-1}\p{E_n}, \end{aligned}x∈f−1(n=1⋃∞​En​)​⟺∃n:f(x)∈En​⟺∃n:x∈f−1(En​)⟺x∈n=1⋃∞​f−1(En​),​

  2. Since X×YX \times YX×Y has the product topology, the coordinate maps πX ⁣:X×Y→X\func{\pi_X}{X \times Y}{X}πX​:X×Y→X and πY ⁣:X×Y→Y\func{\pi_Y}{X \times Y}{Y}πY​:X×Y→Y are continuous.     A×E=(A×Y)∩(X×E)∈B(X×Y),A \times E = \p{A \times Y} \cap \p{X \times E} \in \mathcal{B}\p{X \times Y},A×E=(A×Y)∩(X×E)∈B(X×Y),

    which completes the proof.
