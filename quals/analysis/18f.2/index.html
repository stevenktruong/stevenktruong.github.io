Fall 2018 - Problem 2

Stone-Weierstrass

Let (X,ρ)\p{X, \rho}(X,ρ) be a compact metric space which has at least two points, and let C(X)C\p{X}C(X) be the space of continuous functions X→RX \to \RX→R with the uniform norm ∥f∥=sup⁡X∣f(x)∣\norm{f} = \sup_X \abs{f\p{x}}∥f∥=supX​∣f(x)∣. Let DDD be a dense subset of XXX and for each y∈Dy \in Dy∈D define fy∈C(X)f_y \in C\p{X}fy​∈C(X) by

fy(x)=ρ(x,y).f_y\p{x} = \rho\p{x, y}.fy​(x)=ρ(x,y).

Let AAA be the subalgebra of C(X)C\p{X}C(X) generated by the collection {fy∣y∈D}\set{f_y \mid y \in D}{fy​∣y∈D} (with pointwise addition and multiplication of functions).

  1. Prove that AAA is dense in C(X)C\p{X}C(X) under the uniform norm.
  2. Prove that C(X)C\p{X}C(X) is separable.

    ρ(x,z)≥ρ(x,y)−ρ(y,z)≥δ2>0,\rho\p{x, z} \geq \rho\p{x, y} - \rho\p{y, z} \geq \frac{\delta}{2} > 0,ρ(x,z)≥ρ(x,y)−ρ(y,z)≥2δ​>0,

    so in particular, fz(x)≠0f_z\p{x} \neq 0fz​(x)=0. To show that AAA separates points, let x,y∈Xx, y \in Xx,y∈X be distinct. As before, let δ=ρ(x,y)>0\delta = \rho\p{x, y} > 0δ=ρ(x,y)>0.     ∑k=1nck∏j=1mkfzjk\sum_{k=1}^n c_k \prod_{j=1}^{m_k} f_{z_{jk}}k=1∑n​ck​j=1∏mk​​fzjk​​
