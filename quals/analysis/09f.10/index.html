Fall 2009 - Problem 10

conformal mappings, construction

Consider the unit sphere in R3\R^3R3 with poles removed:

S≔{(cos⁡θsin⁡φ,sin⁡θsin⁡φ,cos⁡φ)∣0<φ<π and θ∈R}.S \coloneqq \set{\p{\cos\theta\sin\phi, \sin\theta\sin\phi, \cos\phi} \mid 0 < \phi < \pi \text{ and } \theta \in \R}.S:={(cosθsinφ,sinθsinφ,cosφ)∣0<φ<π and θ∈R}.

Give an explicit formula for a conformal map from the complex plane onto SSS so that horizontal lines are mapped to circles of constant φ\phiφ and vertical lines are mapped to arcs of constant θ\thetaθ.
Solution.

Let f ⁣:C∖{0}→S\func{f}{\C \setminus \set{0}}{S}f:C∖{0}→S be given by stereographic projection, that is,

f(z)=(2Re⁡z∣z∣2+1,2Im⁡z∣z∣2+1,∣z∣2−1∣z∣2+1),f\p{z} = \p{\frac{2\Re{z}}{\abs{z}^2 + 1}, \frac{2\Im{z}}{\abs{z}^2 + 1}, \frac{\abs{z}^2 - 1}{\abs{z}^2 + 1}},f(z)=(∣z∣2+12Rez​,∣z∣2+12Imz​,∣z∣2+1∣z∣2−1​),

which is a conformal map sending circles centered at the origin to circles of constant φ\phiφ. Notice that fff has inverse

f−1(cos⁡θsin⁡φ,sin⁡θsin⁡φ,cos⁡φ)=cos⁡θsin⁡φ+i(sin⁡θsin⁡φ)1−cos⁡φ=(sin⁡φ1−cos⁡φ)eiθ\begin{aligned} f^{-1}\p{\cos\theta\sin\phi, \sin\theta\sin\phi, \cos\phi} &= \frac{\cos\theta\sin\phi + i\p{\sin\theta\sin\phi}}{1 - \cos\phi} \\ &= \p{\frac{\sin\phi}{1 - \cos\phi}}e^{i\theta} \end{aligned}f−1(cosθsinφ,sinθsinφ,cosφ)​=1−cosφcosθsinφ+i(sinθsinφ)​=(1−cosφsinφ​)eiθ​

From here, we see that an arc of constant θ\thetaθ comes from a straight line passing through the origin that makes an angle θ\thetaθ with the real axis.

To find a map that sends any horizontal line to circles of constant φ\phiφ and vertical lines to arcs of constant θ\thetaθ, it suffices to find a conformal map which:

  • sends horizontal lines to a circle centered at the origin and
  • sends vertical lines to a line passing through the origin.

The map exp⁡ ⁣:C→C∖{0}\func{\exp}{\C}{\C \setminus \set{0}}exp:C→C∖{0} does the trick:

exp⁡(x+iy)=ex(cos⁡y+isin⁡y).\exp\p{x + iy} = e^x\p{\cos{y} + i\sin{y}}.exp(x+iy)=ex(cosy+isiny).

We see that horizontal lines, where xxx is held constant, are mapped to circles of radius exe^xex centered at the origin. Similarly, vertical lines, where yyy is held constant, is mapped to a line which is contained in the line spanned by eiye^{iy}eiy. Thus, f∘exp⁡ ⁣:C→S\func{f \circ \exp}{\C}{S}f∘exp:C→S,

(f∘exp⁡)(z)=(2Re⁡(ez)∣ez∣2+1,2Im⁡(ez)∣ez∣2+1,∣ez∣2−1∣ez∣2+1)\p{f \circ \exp}\p{z} = \p{\frac{2\Re\p{e^z}}{\abs{e^z}^2 + 1}, \frac{2\Im\p{e^z}}{\abs{e^z}^2 + 1}, \frac{\abs{e^z}^2 - 1}{\abs{e^z}^2 + 1}}(f∘exp)(z)=(∣ez∣2+12Re(ez)​,∣ez∣2+12Im(ez)​,∣ez∣2+1∣ez∣2−1​)

is a conformal map with the desired properties.
