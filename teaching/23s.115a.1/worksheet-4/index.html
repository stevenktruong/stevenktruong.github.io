Problem 3

Let V=CV = \CV=C be the vector space of complex numbers over the field R\RR. Define the function T ⁣:V→V\func{T}{V}{V}T:V→V by T(z)=z‾T\p{z} = \conj{z}T(z)=z, where z‾\conj{z}z is the complex conjugate of zzz.

  1. Prove that TTT is additive.
  2. Prove that TTT is linear.
  3. Let β={1,i}\beta = \set{1, i}β={1,i}. Prove that β\betaβ is a basis of VVV over R\RR.
  4. Compute [T]β\br{T}_\beta[T]β​.
Solution.

Complex conjugation is defined as follows: given z∈Cz \in \Cz∈C, we can always write z=a+biz = a + biz=a+bi, where a,b∈Ra, b \in \Ra,b∈R. Then

z‾≔a−bi.\conj{z} \coloneqq a - bi.z:=a−bi.

  1. Let z,w∈Cz, w \in \Cz,w∈C. Then there exist a,b,c,d∈Ra, b, c, d \in \Ra,b,c,d∈R such that z=a+biz = a + biz=a+bi and w=c+diw = c + diw=c+di. Recall that with this notation, addition in C\CC is defined as z+w=(a+c)+(b+d)iz + w = \p{a + c} + \p{b + d}iz+w=(a+c)+(b+d)i. Thus,

    T(z+w)=z+w‾=(a+c)+(b+d)i‾=a+c−(b+d)i=(a−bi)+(c−di)=z‾+w‾=T(z)+T(w).\begin{aligned} T\p{z + w} &= \conj{z + w} \\ &= \conj{\p{a + c} + \p{b + d}i} \\ &= a + c - \p{b + d}i \\ &= \p{a - bi} + \p{c - di} \\ &= \conj{z} + \conj{w} \\ &= T\p{z} + T\p{w}. \end{aligned}T(z+w)​=z+w​=(a+c)+(b+d)i​=a+c−(b+d)i=(a−bi)+(c−di)=z+w=T(z)+T(w).​

  2. Let z=a+bi∈Cz = a + bi \in \Cz=a+bi∈C and let c∈Rc \in \Rc∈R. Note that by definition, cz=ca+cbicz = ca + cbicz=ca+cbi, and that ca,cb∈Rca, cb \in \Rca,cb∈R. Thus,

    T(cz)=cz‾=ca+cbi‾=ca−cbi=c(a−bi)=cz‾=cT(z).\begin{aligned} T\p{cz} &= \conj{cz} \\ &= \overline{ca + cbi} \\ &= ca - cbi \\ &= c\p{a - bi} \\ &= c \conj{z} \\ &= cT\p{z}. \end{aligned}T(cz)​=cz=ca+cbi​=ca−cbi=c(a−bi)=cz=cT(z).​

  3. By definition, β\betaβ spans VVV (every complex number can be written in the form a+bia + bia+bi, where a,b∈Ra, b \in \Ra,b∈R). We only need to show that β\betaβ is linearly independent. Assume that z=a+bi=0z = a + bi = 0z=a+bi=0 for some a,b∈Ra, b \in \Ra,b∈R. Then because TTT is linear, we have T(z)=0T\p{z} = 0T(z)=0. Thus,

    {0=z+T(z)=(a+bi)+(a−bi)=2a,0=z−T(z)=(a+bi)−(a−bi)=2bi.\begin{cases} 0 = z + T\p{z} = \p{a + bi} + \p{a - bi} = 2a, \\ 0 = z - T\p{z} = \p{a + bi} - \p{a - bi} = 2bi. \end{cases}{0=z+T(z)=(a+bi)+(a−bi)=2a,0=z−T(z)=(a+bi)−(a−bi)=2bi.​

    Thus, a=b=0a = b = 0a=b=0 (since 2≠02 \neq 02=0 and i≠0i \neq 0i=0 in C\CC).

  4. Recall that

    [T]β=(∣∣[T(1)]β[T(i)]β∣∣),\br{T}_\beta = \begin{pmatrix} \vert & \vert \\ \br{T\p{1}}_\beta & \br{T\p{i}}_\beta \\ \vert & \vert \end{pmatrix},[T]β​=⎝⎛​∣[T(1)]β​∣​∣[T(i)]β​∣​⎠⎞​,

    so we just need to write T(1)T\p{1}T(1) and T(i)T\p{i}T(i) as linear combinations of β\betaβ:

    T(1)=1=1+0i  ⟹  [T(1)]β=(10)T(i)=−i=0+(−1)i  ⟹  [T(i)]β=(−0−1)\begin{aligned} T\p{1} = 1 = 1 + 0i &\implies \br{T\p{1}}_\beta = \begin{pmatrix} 1 \\ 0 \end{pmatrix} \\ T\p{i} = -i = 0 + \p{-1}i &\implies \br{T\p{i}}_\beta = \begin{pmatrix} \phantom{-}0 \\ -1 \end{pmatrix} \end{aligned}T(1)=1=1+0iT(i)=−i=0+(−1)i​⟹[T(1)]β​=(10​)⟹[T(i)]β​=(−0−1​)​

    Putting everything together,

    [T]β=(1−00−1).\br{T}_\beta = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & \phantom{-}0 \\ 0 & -1 \end{pmatrix}.[T]β​=(10​−0−1​).

Problem 5

Let VVV be a vector space of dimension nnn, let T ⁣:V→V\func{T}{V}{V}T:V→V be a linear function. Suppose that WWW is a TTT-invariant subspace of VVV with dimension kkk. Show that there exists a basis β\betaβ of VVV such that

[T]β=(ABOC),\br{T}_\beta = \begin{pmatrix} A & B \\ O & C \end{pmatrix},[T]β​=(AO​BC​),

where AAA is a k×kk \times kk×k matrix, BBB is a k×(n−k)k \times \p{n - k}k×(n−k) matrix, CCC is an (n−k)×(n−k)\p{n - k} \times \p{n - k}(n−k)×(n−k) matrix, and OOO is the (n−k)×k\p{n - k} \times k(n−k)×k zero matrix.

Solution.

If β={v1,v2,…,vn}\beta = \set{v_1, v_2, \ldots, v_n}β={v1​,v2​,…,vn​} is a basis of VVV, then recall that

[T]β=(∣∣∣[T(v1)]β[T(v2)]β⋯[T(vn)]β∣∣∣).\br{T}_\beta = \begin{pmatrix} \vert & \vert & & \vert \\ \br{T\p{v_1}}_\beta & \br{T\p{v_2}}_\beta & \cdots & \br{T\p{v_n}}_\beta \\ \vert & \vert & & \vert \\ \end{pmatrix}.[T]β​=⎝⎛​∣[T(v1​)]β​∣​∣[T(v2​)]β​∣​⋯​∣[T(vn​)]β​∣​⎠⎞​.

So we need to find a basis β\betaβ such that if 1≤i≤k1 \leq i \leq k1≤i≤k, then

[T(vi)]β=(a1ia2i⋮aki0⋮0)  ⟺  T(vi)=a1iv1+a2iv2+⋯+akivk+0vk+1+⋯+0vn,\br{T\p{v_i}}_\beta = \begin{pmatrix} a_{1i} \\ a_{2i} \\ \vdots \\ a_{ki} \\ 0 \\ \vdots \\ 0 \end{pmatrix} \iff T\p{v_i} = a_{1i} v_1 + a_{2i} v_2 + \cdots + a_{ki} v_k + 0v_{k+1} + \cdots + 0v_n,[T(vi​)]β​=⎝⎛​a1i​a2i​⋮aki​0⋮0​⎠⎞​⟺T(vi​)=a1i​v1​+a2i​v2​+⋯+aki​vk​+0vk+1​+⋯+0vn​,

i.e., a basis such that T(vi)T\p{v_i}T(vi​) can be written as a linear combination of just the first kkk basis vectors.

Let {v1,v2,…,vk}\set{v_1, v_2, \ldots, v_k}{v1​,v2​,…,vk​} be a basis of WWW, and extend this to a basis β={v1,v2,…,vk,vk+1,…,vn}\beta = \set{v_1, v_2, \ldots, v_k, v_{k+1}, \ldots, v_n}β={v1​,v2​,…,vk​,vk+1​,…,vn​} of VVV. Let 1≤i≤k1 \leq i \leq k1≤i≤k. Since WWW is TTT-invariant and vi∈Wv_i \in Wvi​∈W, this means that T(vi)∈WT\p{v_i} \in WT(vi​)∈W. But {v1,v2,…,vk}\set{v_1, v_2, \ldots, v_k}{v1​,v2​,…,vk​} is a basis of WWW, so in particular, W=span⁡{v1,v2,…,vk}W = \span\set{v_1, v_2, \ldots, v_k}W=span{v1​,v2​,…,vk​}. Thus, by definition of span, there exist scalars a1i,a2i,…,aki∈Fa_{1i}, a_{2i}, \ldots, a_{ki} \in \Fa1i​,a2i​,…,aki​∈F such that

T(vi)=a1iv1+a2iv2+⋯+akivk=a1iv1+a2iv2+⋯+akivk+0vk+1+⋯+0vn.\begin{aligned} T\p{v_i} &= a_{1i} v_1 + a_{2i} v_2 + \cdots + a_{ki} v_k \\ &= a_{1i} v_1 + a_{2i} v_2 + \cdots + a_{ki} v_k + 0v_{k+1} + \cdots + 0v_n. \end{aligned}T(vi​)​=a1i​v1​+a2i​v2​+⋯+aki​vk​=a1i​v1​+a2i​v2​+⋯+aki​vk​+0vk+1​+⋯+0vn​.​

Hence, the claim holds with AAA defined by Aij=aijA_{ij} = a_{ij}Aij​=aij​.

Note that we can always write TTT as an n×nn \times nn×n matrix, so we don't need to deal with BBB or CCC since we didn't make any assumptions on the last n−kn - kn−k columns of [T]β\br{T}_\beta[T]β​. We only needed to deal with the first kkk columns and show that they have the special form above.
