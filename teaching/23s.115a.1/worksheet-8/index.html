Problem 3

Let A∈Mn×n(F)A \in M_{n \times n}\p{\F}A∈Mn×n​(F) be similar to an upper-triangular matrix, and suppose that AAA has distinct eigenvalues λ1,…,λk\lambda_1, \ldots, \lambda_kλ1​,…,λk​ with corresponding algebraic multiplicities m1,…,mkm_1, \ldots, m_km1​,…,mk​.

  1. Prove that tr⁡A=∑i=1kmiλi\tr A = \sum_{i=1}^k m_i\lambda_itrA=∑i=1k​mi​λi​.
  2. Prove that det⁡A=∏i=1kλimi\det A = \prod_{i=1}^k \lambda_i^{m_i}detA=∏i=1k​λimi​​.
Solution.

This follows from two facts:

  1. If AAA and BBB are similar, then they have the same eigenvalues.
  2. The eigenvalues of an upper-triangular matrix are its diagonal entries.

Fact 1 follows from the fact that if B=QAQ−1B = QAQ^{-1}B=QAQ−1, then

det⁡(B−λI)=det⁡(Q(A−λI)Q−1)=det⁡(A−λI),\det\p{B - \lambda I} = \det\p{Q\p{A - \lambda I}Q^{-1}} = \det\p{A - \lambda I},det(B−λI)=det(Q(A−λI)Q−1)=det(A−λI),

i.e., AAA and BBB have the same characteristic equation. For Fact 2, if AAA is upper-triangular with diagonal entries a1,…,ana_1, \ldots, a_na1​,…,an​, then because A−λIA - \lambda IA−λI is also upper-triangular with diagonal entries a1−λ,…,an−λa_1 - \lambda, \ldots, a_n - \lambdaa1​−λ,…,an​−λ, the characteristic equation of AAA is

pA(λ)=det⁡(A−λI)=∏i=1n(ai−λ).p_A\p{\lambda} = \det\p{A - \lambda I} = \prod_{i=1}^n \p{a_i - \lambda}.pA​(λ)=det(A−λI)=i=1∏n​(ai​−λ).

Thus, the roots of pAp_ApA​ are exactly the diagonal entries, so the eigenvalues of AAA are also just the diagonal entries.

  1. Note that tr⁡(AB)=tr⁡(BA)\tr\p{AB} = \tr\p{BA}tr(AB)=tr(BA) from a simple calculation:

    tr⁡(AB)=∑i=1n(AB)ii=∑i=1n∑j=1nAijBji=∑j=1n∑i=1nBjiAij=∑j=1n(BA)jj=tr⁡(BA).\begin{aligned} \tr\p{AB} = \sum_{i=1}^n \p{AB}_{ii} &= \sum_{i=1}^n \sum_{j=1}^n A_{ij} B_{ji} \\ &= \sum_{j=1}^n \sum_{i=1}^n B_{ji} A_{ij} \\ &= \sum_{j=1}^n \p{BA}_{jj} \\ &= \tr\p{BA}. \end{aligned}tr(AB)=i=1∑n​(AB)ii​​=i=1∑n​j=1∑n​Aij​Bji​=j=1∑n​i=1∑n​Bji​Aij​=j=1∑n​(BA)jj​=tr(BA).​

    Thus, if A=QUQ−1A = QUQ^{-1}A=QUQ−1, where UUU is an upper-triangular matrix, then

    tr⁡A=tr⁡(QUQ−1)=tr⁡(Q−1QU)=tr⁡U.\tr A = \tr\p{QUQ^{-1}} = \tr\p{Q^{-1}QU} = \tr U.trA=tr(QUQ−1)=tr(Q−1QU)=trU.

    But the diagonal entries of UUU are the eigenvalues of AAA counted with multiplicity, so

    tr⁡U=∑i=1kmiλi.\tr U = \sum_{i=1}^k m_i\lambda_i.trU=i=1∑k​mi​λi​.

  2. Again, write A=QUQ−1A = QUQ^{-1}A=QUQ−1, where UUU is upper-triangular. Then the determinant of UUU is just the product of the diagonal entries, which are the eigenvalues of AAA counted with multiplicity. Thus,

    det⁡A=det⁡U=∏i=1kλimi.\det A = \det U = \prod_{i=1}^k \lambda_i^{m_i}.detA=detU=i=1∏k​λimi​​.

Problem 4

Let VVV be a finite dimensional vector space over F\FF, let T∈L(V)T \in \mathcal{L}\p{V}T∈L(V) be invertible.

  1. Prove that if λ\lambdaλ is an eigenvalue of TTT, then λ−1\lambda^{-1}λ−1 is an eigenvalue of T−1T^{-1}T−1.
  2. Prove that the eigenspace of TTT corresponding to λ\lambdaλ is the same as the eigenspace of T−1T^{-1}T−1 corresponding to λ−1\lambda^{-1}λ−1.
  3. Prove that if TTT is diagonalizable, then T−1T^{-1}T−1 is diagonalizable.
Solution.

These all follow from the fact that vvv is an eigenvector for TTT if and only if it's an eigenvector for T−1T^{-1}T−1. If vvv is an eigenvector for TTT with eigenvalue λ\lambdaλ, then

T(v)=λv  ⟹  v=λT−1(v)  ⟹  T−1(v)=λ−1v.T\p{v} = \lambda v \implies v = \lambda T^{-1}\p{v} \implies T^{-1}\p{v} = \lambda^{-1} v.T(v)=λv⟹v=λT−1(v)⟹T−1(v)=λ−1v.

Note that because TTT is invertible, λ≠0\lambda \neq 0λ=0, so λ−1\lambda^{-1}λ−1 is well-defined.

Since vvv is an eigenvector of TTT, we know that v≠0v \neq 0v=0, so this precisely says that vvv is an eigenvector for TTT with eigenvalue λ−1\lambda^{-1}λ−1. Applying the forward direction with T,λT, \lambdaT,λ replaced with T−1,λ−1T^{-1}, \lambda^{-1}T−1,λ−1 and noting that (T−1)−1=T\p{T^{-1}}^{-1} = T(T−1)−1=T and (λ−1)−1=λ\p{\lambda^{-1}}^{-1} = \lambda(λ−1)−1=λ gives the reverse direction. (Alternatively, you can just do the same computation, but in reverse.)

  1. Our computation above shows that if λ\lambdaλ is an eigenvalue for TTT, then λ−1\lambda^{-1}λ−1 is an eigenvalue for T−1T^{-1}T−1.

  2. Recall that the eigenspace of TTT corresponding to λ\lambdaλ is the set

    Eλ(T)={v∈V∣T(v)=λv}.E_\lambda\p{T} = \set{v \in V \mid T\p{v} = \lambda v}.Eλ​(T)={v∈V∣T(v)=λv}.

    We showed that T(v)=λvT\p{v} = \lambda vT(v)=λv if and only if T−1(v)=λ−1vT^{-1}\p{v} = \lambda^{-1} vT−1(v)=λ−1v, and this implies Eλ(T)=Eλ−1(T−1)E_\lambda\p{T} = E_{\lambda^{-1}}\p{T^{-1}}Eλ​(T)=Eλ−1​(T−1).

  3. Let β={v1,…,vn}\beta = \set{v_1, \ldots, v_n}β={v1​,…,vn​} be an eigenbasis of VVV for TTT. Then β\betaβ is also a set of eigenvectors for T−1T^{-1}T−1. Since β\betaβ was a basis of VVV, this means that β\betaβ is also an eigenbasis of VVV for T−1T^{-1}T−1.
