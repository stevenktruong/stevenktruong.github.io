Problem 5

Let VVV and WWW be finite-dimensional vector spaces and T ⁣:V→W\func{T}{V}{W}T:V→W be a linear function.

  1. Prove that if dim⁡V<dim⁡W\dim V < \dim WdimV<dimW, then TTT cannot be surjective.
  2. Prove that if dim⁡V>dim⁡W\dim V > \dim WdimV>dimW, then TTT cannot be injective.
Solution.

Since VVV is finite-dimensional and TTT is linear, we may use the rank-nullity theorem, which tells us that

dim⁡R(T)+dim⁡N(T)=dim⁡V.\dim R\p{T} + \dim N\p{T} = \dim V.dimR(T)+dimN(T)=dimV.

Here, R(T)R\p{T}R(T) is the range of TTT and N(T)N\p{T}N(T) is the null-space (or kernel) of TTT.

  1. Since dim⁡V<dim⁡W\dim V < \dim WdimV<dimW and 0≤dim⁡N(T)0 \leq \dim N\p{T}0≤dimN(T), we get

    dim⁡R(T)≤dim⁡R(T)+dim⁡N(T)=dim⁡V<dim⁡W.\begin{aligned} \dim R\p{T} &\leq \dim R\p{T} + \dim N\p{T} \\ &= \dim V \\ &< \dim W. \end{aligned}dimR(T)​≤dimR(T)+dimN(T)=dimV<dimW.​

    If TTT were surjective, then R(T)=W  ⟹  dim⁡R(T)=dim⁡WR\p{T} = W \implies \dim R\p{T} = \dim WR(T)=W⟹dimR(T)=dimW, which is impossible.

  2. Since R(T)⊆WR\p{T} \subseteq WR(T)⊆W, we know dim⁡R(T)≤dim⁡W\dim R\p{T} \leq \dim WdimR(T)≤dimW. Thus, by rank-nullity,

    dim⁡N(T)=dim⁡V−dim⁡R(T)≥dim⁡V−dim⁡W>0.\begin{aligned} \dim N\p{T} &= \dim V - \dim R\p{T} \\ &\geq \dim V - \dim W \\ &> 0. \end{aligned}dimN(T)​=dimV−dimR(T)≥dimV−dimW>0.​

    Thus, N(T)N\p{T}N(T) is non-trivial (i.e., N(T)≠{0}N\p{T} \neq \set{0}N(T)={0}), so TTT cannot be injective.
