    (dℓ1(x,y))2=(∑i=1n∣xi−yi∣)2=∑i=1n∣xi−yi∣2+∑i≠j∣xi−yi∣∣xj−yj∣⏟≥0≥∑i=1n∣xi−yi∣2=(dℓ2(x,y))2.\begin{aligned} \p{d_{\ell^1}\p{x, y}}^2 = \p{\sum_{i=1}^n \abs{x_i - y_i}}^2 &= \sum_{i=1}^n \abs{x_i - y_i}^2 + \underbrace{\sum_{i \neq j} \abs{x_i - y_i} \abs{x_j - y_j}}_{\geq 0} \\ &\geq \sum_{i=1}^n \abs{x_i - y_i}^2 \\ &= \p{d_{\ell^2}\p{x, y}}^2. \end{aligned}(dℓ1​(x,y))2=(i=1∑n​∣xi​−yi​∣)2​=i=1∑n​∣xi​−yi​∣2+≥0i=j∑​∣xi​−yi​∣∣xj​−yj​∣​​≥i=1∑n​∣xi​−yi​∣2=(dℓ2​(x,y))2.​

    dℓ2(x,y)≤dℓ1(x,y).d_{\ell^2}\p{x, y} \leq d_{\ell^1}\p{x, y}.dℓ2​(x,y)≤dℓ1​(x,y).

    In particular, if lim⁡k→∞dℓ1(x(k),x(0))=0\lim_{k\to\infty} d_{\ell^1}\p{x^{\p{k}}, x^{\p{0}}} = 0limk→∞​dℓ1​(x(k),x(0))=0, then by the squeeze theorem,

    ∣xi−yi∣≤∑j=1n∣xj−yj∣=dℓ1(x,y).\abs{x_i - y_i} \leq \sum_{j=1}^n \abs{x_j - y_j} = d_{\ell^1}\p{x, y}.∣xi​−yi​∣≤j=1∑n​∣xj​−yj​∣=dℓ1​(x,y).

Problem 3

Prove Proposition 1.2.10.

Proposition (1.2.10)

Let (X,d)\p{X, d}(X,d) be a metric space, let EEE be a subset of XXX, and let x0x_0x0​ be a point in XXX. Then the following statements are logically equivalent.

  1. x0x_0x0​ is an adherent point of EEE.
  2. x0x_0x0​ is either an interior point or a boundary point of EEE.
  3. There exists a sequence (xn)n=1∞\p{x_n}_{n=1}^\infty(xn​)n=1∞​ in EEE which converges to x0x_0x0​ with respect to the metric ddd.
Solution.

We will show (i)   ⟺  \iff⟺ (ii), and then (i)   ⟹  \implies⟹ (iii)   ⟹  \implies⟹ (i).

(i)   ⟺  \iff⟺ (ii):

Recall that X=Int⁡(E)∪∂E∪Ext⁡(E)X = \Int\p{E} \cup \partial E \cup \Ext\p{E}X=Int(E)∪∂E∪Ext(E) and that this is a disjoint union. Thus, it suffices to show that x0∉Ext⁡(E)x_0 \notin \Ext\p{E}x0​∈/Ext(E). Notice that

(∀r>0:B(x0,r)∩E≠∅)  ⟺  ¬(∃r>0:B(x0,r)∩E=∅).\p{\forall r > 0 : B\p{x_0, r} \cap E \neq \emptyset} \iff \neg \p{\exists r > 0 : B\p{x_0, r} \cap E = \emptyset}.(∀r>0:B(x0​,r)∩E=∅)⟺¬(∃r>0:B(x0​,r)∩E=∅).

In other words,

x0 is an adherent point  ⟺  x∉Ext⁡(E).x_0\text{ is an adherent point} \iff x \notin \Ext\p{E}.x0​ is an adherent point⟺x∈/Ext(E).

Problem 5

Prove Proposition 1.3.4(b).

Proposition (1.3.4)

Let (X,d)\p{X, d}(X,d) be a metric space, let YYY be a subset of XXX, and let EEE be a subset of YYY.

  1. EEE is relatively open with respect to YYY if and only if E=V∩YE = V \cap YE=V∩Y for some set V⊆XV \subseteq XV⊆X which is open in XXX.
  2. EEE is relatively closed with respect to YYY if and only if E=K∩YE = K \cap YE=K∩Y for some set K⊆XK \subseteq XK⊆X which is closed in XXX.
Solution.

While Solution 1 is much easier than Solution 2, I highly recommend that you understand Solution 2 as well since it requires more understanding of the metric space definitions--it's very good practice.

Throughout this problem, I'll be using Proposition 1.2.15 (the long one) many times without mentioning it explicitly.

Solution 1 (using part (i))

"  ⟹  \implies⟹"

Assume EEE is relatively closed with respect to YYY. Then Y∖EY \setminus EY∖E is open in YYY, so by part (i), there exists V⊆XV \subseteq XV⊆X which is open in XXX such that Y∖E=V∩YY \setminus E = V \cap YY∖E=V∩Y. Notice that

E=Y∖(Y∖E)=Y∩(X∖(V∩Y))=Y∩((X∖V)∪(X∖Y))=(Y∩(X∖V))∪(Y∩(X∖Y))⏟=∅=Y∩(X∖V).\begin{aligned} E &= Y \setminus \p{Y \setminus E} \\ &= Y \cap \p{X \setminus \p{V \cap Y}} \\ &= Y \cap \p{\p{X \setminus V} \cup \p{X \setminus Y}} \\ &= \p{Y \cap \p{X \setminus V}} \cup \underbrace{\p{Y \cap \p{X \setminus Y}}}_{=\emptyset} \\ &= Y \cap \p{X \setminus V}. \end{aligned}E​=Y∖(Y∖E)=Y∩(X∖(V∩Y))=Y∩((X∖V)∪(X∖Y))=(Y∩(X∖V))∪=∅(Y∩(X∖Y))​​=Y∩(X∖V).​

Since VVV was open in XXX, it follows that K≔X∖VK \coloneqq X \setminus VK:=X∖V is closed in XXX.

"  ⟸  \impliedby⟸"

Assume that E=K∩YE = K \cap YE=K∩Y for some set K⊆XK \subseteq XK⊆X which is closed in XXX. Then by a similar computation as above,

Y∖E=Y∖(K∩Y)=Y∩(X∖K).Y \setminus E = Y \setminus \p{K \cap Y} = Y \cap \p{X \setminus K}.Y∖E=Y∖(K∩Y)=Y∩(X∖K).

Since KKK is closed in XXX, V≔X∖KV \coloneqq X \setminus KV:=X∖K is open in XXX. Thus, part (i), Y∖EY \setminus EY∖E is open in YYY, and hence EEE is closed in YYY.

"  ⟹  \implies⟹"

"  ⟸  \impliedby⟸"

Let y0∈Yy_0 \in Yy0​∈Y be an adherent point of EEE in (Y,dY×Y)\p{Y, d_{Y \times Y}}(Y,dY×Y​). Since E=K∩YE = K \cap YE=K∩Y, we only need to show that y0∈Ky_0 \in Ky0​∈K.
