Problem 1

A bridge hand is found by taking 131313 cards at random and without replacement from a standard deck of 525252 cards. Find the probability of drawing each of the following hands.

  1. One in which there are five spades, two hearts, two diamonds, and four clubs.
  2. One in which there are at least three spades, at least three hearts, at least three diamonds, and at least three clubs.
Solution.

  1. There are (5213)\binom{52}{13}(1352​) ways to pick 131313 cards, and these are all equally likely. There are 131313 cards of each suit, so there are (135)\binom{13}{5}(513​) ways to pick five spades, (132)\binom{13}{2}(213​) ways to pick two hearts, etc., so the final answer is

    (135)(132)(132)(134)(5213).\boxed{\frac{\binom{13}{5} \binom{13}{2} \binom{13}{2} \binom{13}{4}}{\binom{52}{13}}}.(1352​)(513​)(213​)(213​)(413​)​​.

  2. The condition just tells us that our hand should have 333 cards for 333 of the suits, and 444 cards for the final suit. So, we need to pick the suit that gets 444 cards, and then pick our cards accordingly. We get

    (41)(134)(133)(133)(133)(5213).\boxed{\binom{4}{1} \frac{\binom{13}{4} \binom{13}{3} \binom{13}{3} \binom{13}{3}}{\binom{52}{13}}}.(14​)(1352​)(413​)(313​)(313​)(313​)​​.

Problem 3

A hospital receives 70%70\%70% of its flu vaccine from Company Good and the remainder from Company Evil. Each shipment contains a large vial of vaccine. From Company Good, 10%10\%10% of the vials are ineffective. From Company Evil, 80%80\%80% are ineffective. A hospital tests n=10n = 10n=10 randomly selected vials from one shipment for their effectiveness.

  1. Compute the probability that exactly two of these 101010 vials are ineffective, provided this shipment comes from Company Good.
  2. Compute the probability that exactly two of these 101010 vials are ineffective, provided this shipment comes from Company Evil.
  3. Compute the probability that exactly two of these 101010 vials are ineffective.
  4. Compute the probability that this shipment comes from Company Good, provided exactly two of these 101010 vials are ineffective.
Solution.

Let

A={exactly 2 vials are ineffective}G={the shipment is from Company Good}E={the shipment is from Company Evil}.\begin{aligned} A &= \set{\text{exactly }2\text{ vials are ineffective}} \\ G &= \set{\text{the shipment is from Company Good}} \\ E &= \set{\text{the shipment is from Company Evil}}. \end{aligned}AGE​={exactly 2 vials are ineffective}={the shipment is from Company Good}={the shipment is from Company Evil}.​

  1. If we assume GGG, then the number of ineffective vials is a binomial random variable with 101010 trials and success probability 0.10.10.1. Thus, the probability is

    P(A | G)=(102)(0.1)2(0.9)8.\boxed{\P\p{A \given G} = \binom{10}{2} \p{0.1}^2 \p{0.9}^8}.P(A∣G)=(210​)(0.1)2(0.9)8​.

  2. This is the same as (1), except the success probability is 0.80.80.8 this time, so

    P(A | E)=(102)(0.8)2(0.2)8.\boxed{\P\p{A \given E} = \binom{10}{2} \p{0.8}^2 \p{0.2}^8}.P(A∣E)=(210​)(0.8)2(0.2)8​.

    P(A)=P(A∩G)+P(A∩E)=P(G)P(A | G)+P(E)P(A | E)=0.7×(102)(0.1)2(0.9)8+0.3×(102)(0.8)2(0.2)8.\begin{aligned} \P\p{A} &= \P\p{A \cap G} + \P\p{A \cap E} \\ &= \P\p{G} \P\p{A \given G} + \P\p{E} \P\p{A \given E} \\ &= \boxed{0.7 \times \binom{10}{2} \p{0.1}^2 \p{0.9}^8 + 0.3 \times \binom{10}{2} \p{0.8}^2 \p{0.2}^8}. \end{aligned}P(A)​=P(A∩G)+P(A∩E)=P(G)P(A∣G)+P(E)P(A∣E)=0.7×(210​)(0.1)2(0.9)8+0.3×(210​)(0.8)2(0.2)8​.​
