Problem 1

To determine whether the bacteria count was lower in the west basin of Lake Macatawa than in the east basin, n=37n = 37n=37 samples of water were taken from the west basin and the number of bacteria colonies in 100100100 milliliters of water was counted. The sample characteristics were x‾=11.95\mean{x} = 11.95x=11.95 and s=11.80s = 11.80s=11.80, measured in hundreds of colonies. Find an approximate 95%95\%95% confidence interval for the mean number of colonies (say, μW\mu_WμW​) in 100100100 milliliters of water in the west basin.

Solution.

While the true standard deviation is unknown, the sample size is large enough to allow us to use the central limit theorem, so the confidence interval will be
