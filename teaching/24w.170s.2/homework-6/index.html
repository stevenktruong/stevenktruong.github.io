Problem 1

Let XXX represent the tire pressure of a Formula 1 car at its peak temperature, measured in PSI. With the current tires, it is known that X∼N(32,4)X \sim \mathcal{N}\p{32, 4}X∼N(32,4). z=35−322/36=9.z = \frac{35 - 32}{2/\sqrt{36}} = 9.z=2/36​35−32​=9.

  1. The ppp-value is the probability of observing x‾=35\mean{x} = 35x=35 or something more extreme (i.e., numbers that give more evidence that μ>32\mu > 32μ>32). In this problem, this is
